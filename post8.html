Midterm: Lemur Buddy

26 March 2019



Context

Ring-tailed lemurs are highly sociable animals that demonstrate a complex communication system within their highly hierarchically structured groups. These so called lemur troops are female dominated and gender-based or age-based social behaviors emerge and display variously around that central theme. Male Lemurs don‚Äôt have as high social regards as their female counterparts.



One of these behaviors is the Allo-grooming which is seen as a highly-social and selective behavior. Male Lemurs at the bottom of of the society are sometimes left out, which turns them into ‚ÄòLone Riders‚Äô, traveling between different troops. As grooming is a mutual social behavior, we realized that, potentially, male Ring-tailed lemurs in the troop might not get as much responsive mutual interaction for an activity like grooming.


Proposal

We are proposing Lemur Buddy - an allogrooming device for the left-out male Lemurs within Lemur troops. Lemur Buddy shall create a sense of companionship with behavioral feedback and olfactory stimuli that is intuitive and natural to the Ring-tailed Lemurs.


  • Lemur Buddy purrs when a lemur touches it.
  • Lemur Buddy grooms a lemur when lemur rubs through the rollers and the comb wall.
  • Lemur Buddy incorporates olfactory stimulation.

Also, Lemur Buddy is based on and imitates the Lemur‚Äôs grooming posture and has a fluid shape so Lemurs can hold it or hug it with comfort and familiarity.
